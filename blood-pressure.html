Understanding Blood Pressure

What is Blood Pressure?

Blood pressure is the force of blood pushing against the walls of your arteries. It's measured in millimeters of mercury (mmHg) and consists of two numbers:

Systolic (Top Number)

The pressure when your heart beats and pumps blood

Normal: 90-120 mmHg

Diastolic (Bottom Number)

The pressure when your heart rests between beats

Normal: 60-80 mmHg

How to Measure Blood Pressure

1

Prepare

Avoid caffeine, exercise, and smoking for 30 minutes before measuring.

2

Get Comfortable

Sit in a quiet place with your back supported and feet flat on the floor.

3

Position

Rest your arm on a table at heart level. Place the cuff on your bare upper arm.

4

Measure

Stay still and quiet while the measurement is being taken.

5

Record

Note both numbers and any relevant factors (time, activity, etc.).

Important Tips

  • Take measurements at the same time each day
  • Wait 5 minutes between readings if taking multiple measurements
  • Use the same arm for consistency
  • Record any unusual symptoms or circumstances
